Mother's Milk

Installation November 2016

In a class studying Marcel Duchamp, specifily his installation art. I was tasked with creating my own instaltion, as part of a group show in a gallery space called Room 9. Mother's Milk is a project that documents my mother's, Carolynne Gilbert, life.

The prompt

For this final project we were given the ultimate freedom to choose our own direction. The prompt was quite literally "create a group show with your classmates". Were challenged to push ourselves in terms of what we felt free to create and express.

With so much freedom in this project a challenge I had to over come was coming up with my initial idea. After a few brainstorming sessions over some tea, I remembered my mom‚Äôs scrapbooks, and how her mom made scrapbooks of her. Finding this connection intriguing, I went to work sketching out how I could make this idea into a encompassing installation. Then I hit Home Depot for a 8ft x 4ft piece of particleboard to create my custom shelf. I subsequently painted the self white, to match the wall of the gallery.




The final porduct

My final installation was a bookshelf acting as a timeline. The very left is when Carolynne Gilbert, was born October 12, 1971 and the very right February 7, 2013 is the date she passed away. The bookshelf is divided by two dividers, each representing two major life changing events in her life.

The first divider (left) represents giving birth to her ‚Äúunplanned‚Äù first son, Colby Gilbert on March 17, 1995. And the second divider (right) is when she was diagnosed with breast cancer, on February 2, 2005. The green books are about Colby and the blue books about her second son Chanse Gilbert, born December 13, 1998.
