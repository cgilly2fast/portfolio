Out of the Box

SculptureSeptember 2016

A process based piece that forced me to think "Out of the Box".

The process

At the begining of this project I constructed a 4 face 12x12x12 cube using ¾ inch plywood. Each mating edge had 3 screws that were countersinked so the screws are flush with the wood. The project contraint that forced me to minimal processing of the cude for the maximal change cof the cude.

For my transformation, minimal process truly meant minimal process. Putting extraordinary constraints on the process of a work is one of my favorite methods in creating work. It forces "Out of the Box" thinking that is very difficult to recreate when unlimited possibilities lay in front of an artist. For my project I made 2 cuts to remove the corner of my box and then moved the cut out corner and created a stand of sorts for the remaining shape to stand in a way that is abnormal to the viewer.

Because everything is above the fold, however, the page is quite busy and cluttered, making it difficult for users to find what they’re looking for. We used our findings from analytics and user feedback on the old Qantas homepage to help inform our new design.

The influence

My biggest in fleunce in the piece was from Andrea Zittel's A-Z Enterprise. I see myself as a designer with art influences.Her work especially Rough Furniture got me thinking about what is necessary in a house and how that could look differently from conventional standards.

This is evident in my work because many artist feel they must do so much to create a thoughtful work when simplicity is equally capable of the same effects. Her A-Z enterprises work made me think about where my piece would be located outside the classroom and my answer would be a home because this isn't a form to understand racism or take on some big social issue but a form to bring about calmness and contentedness.
