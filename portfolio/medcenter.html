UW Medical Center Show

Art Installation February 2017

The UW Medical Center Show is an annual show in the Sky Gallery at the medical center. Each year the show is put on by the junior class of the Photomedia Program at the University of Washington. This year the show was up for patients to see for a month.

the final product

The UW Medical Center, like for many, is a place where my family has spent a lot of time. My mother who was diagnosed with breast cancer in the February 2005, would drive weekly up to Seattle from Vancouver, WA., for rounds of experimental chemotherapy. This was pretty late in her 8 year battle with the disease (She died February 7th 2013).

The Medical Center was a place of hope, the last hope, and deep sadness for my mom and dad. Although I personally never traveled to the Medical center, but from someone who understands the emotional toll a family member's illness takes on the patient and their family. I intend this work as a “Me Too” to all the patients here. This quilt was made by my grandmother (dad’s mom) early in her fight. My grandma wanted to give my mom something to comfort her in the months to eventual years of attempted rest and recovery from nearly non-stop treatment and surgeries.

My mom appreciated the quilt but did not like the pink color, as constant reminder of her illness. She despised the idea of being defined by breast cancer. I want this quilt to represent how many patients may feel while being at the hospital, being grateful for all that it does but also frustrated by its necessity.
