Wallet Backup

Conceptual ArtworkMay 2018

A tokenized artwork, I blend the speculative markets of cryptocurrency and art to create a decentralized backup system for my bitcoin wallet.

Starting Questions

As I crept towards my transition to a professional artist I slowly learned about the economics of the art market. Some fundamental questions always arise:

  • How much is my art worth?
  • What makes a certain work better, compared to any other work?
  • What makes artwork selling for millions of dollars worth that much?
  • How does an artwork become worthy of being in a museum, gallery, or collection?
  • What makes an artwork increase in value over time?

Although these questions can be answered to some degree, all of the answers are speculative and opinionative.

How It Works

Wallet Backup is a system to ensure access to my bitcoin wallet by piggybacking off the archival practices of an investment collector. This work is a conceptual piece that is manifested in the form of a whitepaper, an agreement contract, and prints that constitute cold storage of my bitcoin wallet.

The work is not in the realization of an object, but simply a facilitator as a means to draw attention to archival practices. If collectors buy artwork as an investment â€” why not skip right to work that acts as a security system to ensure value?

If I lose access to my wallet, a collector reward of two new editions of backups will be given, free of charge, to the collector that successfully returns my credentials. These rewarded backups can be sold, traded, or gifted at the collectorâ€™s discretion.

It's about subverting archival practices. I want to insert myself into that practice for the mutual benefit of my patrons and myself.

Collector Reward

The collect is acting as the miner in this system. When a collector successfully informs me of my backup credentials the collector will be rewarded with two editions of the work at no expense to the collector. The collector has full rights to the piece after signing the Terms & Conditions and may sell, trade, gift, etc. the piece as desired. The piece will be shipped to the collector at no expense to the collector.

Overview Of Back Structure

I offered sixty-four of the one hundred twenty-eight Wallet Backups to be created for purchase by the public in the Colby Gilbert Initial Work Offering (IWO) while a further 50% of the Wallet Backups to be created will be retained to be distributed for purpose of the Collector Reward.

Initial Work Offering
